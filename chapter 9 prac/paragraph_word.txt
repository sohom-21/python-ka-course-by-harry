the faint scent of cinnamon and burnt sugar lingered in the air, a ghostly reminder of the bakery that had closed down months ago. outside, a lone cyclist battled a headwind, her bright yellow jacket a stark contrast to the muted grey of the overcast sky. pigeons huddled on the ledge of the old municipal building, occasionally shifting positions with a soft flutter of wings. somewhere down the street, a car alarm began its repetitive, irritating cry, only to be abruptly silenced moments later, leaving the slightly damp quietude to settle back over the afternoon.
